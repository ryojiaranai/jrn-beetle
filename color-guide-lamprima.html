Lamprima Stag Beetle

Lamprima adolphinae — One of the beetles in our collection

Quick Info

  • Scientific Name: Lamprima adolphinae
  • Family: Lucanidae (Stag Beetles)
  • Common Names: Jewel Stag Beetle
  • Origin: New Guinea (montane cloud forests)
  • Size: Males 24–60mm, Females 18–24mm
  • Color: Metallic structural coloration similar to Rainbow Stag Beetle

Our Morphs

These are the Lamprima color forms we currently keep at JRN BEETLE.

Maziora
Multi-color shifting metallic effect — changes color depending on viewing angle. Named after the Maziora color-shifting paint. One of the most visually stunning forms.
Blue
Rare metallic blue form. Blue is one of the least common and most sought-after colors in Lamprima.

Quick Care

18–24 °C
Temperature
70–80%
Humidity
6–8 Months
Life Cycle

Lamprima is a montane species, so keep them on the cooler side. They do well in flake soil or decaying hardwood substrate.
