Color Morphs

All color lines originate from the Normal morph — the wild-type red-green iridescent form. Through selective breeding over generations, breeders have developed distinct color lines, each with their own progression path.

Normal (White Eyes specimen)
ORIGIN — ALL LINES START HERE

Normal

The wild-type coloration. Red and green iridescent colors appear simultaneously across the elytra in roughly equal proportion. This is the base form from which all selective color lines are developed.

Elytra Red-green balanced mix
Chest Golden
Trait Strength Strongest — appears most when crossbreeding unknown lines

Blue Crest (Lapis)

A deep, dark blue coloration that takes one of the longest times to develop through selective breeding. Japanese breeders call the highest grade of this color "Lapis" — named after the precious stone lapis lazuli. Features a distinctive matte golden chest.

Elytra Deep dark blue

Pikal (Shiny Chest)

First developed by Japanese breeder RTN

Pikal is a special trait — a shiny, metallic chest — that can be combined with almost any color line. Named by their chest color, not their elytra color. Every color variation can potentially have the Pikal trait, except Blue Crest which has not yet been observed with Pikal.

3 Basic Pikal Lines

Special Eye Colors

Rare genetic abnormalities can produce special eye colors. These traits are extremely rare and highly valued, especially when combined with rare body colors.

White Eyes

Rarity

The least rare of the special eye colors, but still uncommon. Value increases dramatically when combined with rare body colors — for example, a white-eyed highest-grade Blue Crest (Lapis) would be extremely difficult to find.

Pink Eyes

Rarity

The most rare eye color for rainbow stag beetles. Breeding methods include crossing white eyes × red eyes, or long-term inbreeding of white eye lines.

Known color combinations: Navy Blue Purple Amber Aurora ⭐ (Rarest!)
JRN BEETLE has the Aurora + Pink Eyes — one of the rarest combinations in existence!

Red Eyes

Rarity

Equally rare as pink eyes. Difficult to see with the naked eye under normal lighting — you need to photograph them under bright light to confirm the red eye color.

Known color combinations: Blue Crest Navy Blue Purple Super Green ⭐ (by Takuma)

Special Forms

Beyond color and eye variations, rainbow stag beetles can also exhibit rare physical form mutations that affect their body shape. These are separate from color traits and can appear in any color line.

Navy Blue Purple Pikal — Thick Horn

SPECIAL FORM — RARE

Thick Horn

A special physical trait where the mandibles (horns) of the male beetle grow noticeably thicker than normal. Developed through selective breeding — breeders pick the thickest-horned males each generation and keep breeding them, gradually increasing horn thickness over multiple generations.

Trait Type Physical form — selective breeding
Affected Part Male mandibles — thicker than standard
Method Select thickest male each generation and breed forward
Combinable Can appear in any color + pikal + eye combination

Breeding Strategy

🧬

Same Line = High Success

Breeding within the same color line gives a very high percentage of offspring matching the parent color — even across different bloodlines. This is the safest approach.

Purple ♂ × Purple ♀
→ ~100% Purple
⚠️

Cross Line = Normal Reversion

Crossing different color lines (e.g., Red × Purple) will produce a high percentage of Normal offspring in F1. But if you continue breeding to CBF2-3, the F0 colors will reappear.

Red ♂ × Purple ♀
→ F1: Mostly Normal
→ F2-3: Red Purple colors return
⚖️

Both Parents Matter Equally

Male and female contribute equally to offspring color. Neither sex has more influence — bloodline quality matters far more than which parent is which sex.

📊

Stabilization Timeline

2-3 generations to stabilize a color line (depends on bloodline compatibility). A line is considered starting to stabilize at 50-60% offspring matching target color.

F2
F3+
Low match Stabilizing Stable

Note: Multi-color morphs like Aurora may always produce ~3 variations even when stable.

🌡️

Environment Matters (A Little)

Temperature, humidity, and substrate can shift colors slightly since rainbow stag beetle coloration is structural (not pigment-based). However, genetics and bloodline are by far the primary factors.
